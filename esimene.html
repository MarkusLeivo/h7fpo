You can try meditation:


  • Find yourself a comfortable place to sit
  • Close your eyes (or leave them slightly open if you prefer)
  • Take a few deep breaths
  • Take a few moments to loosen your body from ypur head to your toes
  • Take a few more slow breaths
  • Stop to notice the sensations throughout your body — the warmth, the coolness or any discomfort.
    Be aware of them, but don't let them concern you too much.
  • Pick one sensation — such as the feeling of your breath going in and out — and devote your attention to it.
    Just focus on that.
  • When your mind wanders, bring your attention back to the breath. After a few moments, your mind may wander again.
    Once again, notice that and simply return your attention back to the present moment.
  • When you're ready — after one minute, 10 minutes or 30 minutes — get your attention back to the present, open your eyes.




You can try meditative jouraling:


  • Get yourself a couple of sheets of paper and a pen
  • Find a space where you won't be disturbed. Then you can set a boundary for
    the practice—either writing for a set length of time or for a set number of pages.
  • Then simply begin to note down whatever is in your mind. Don't worry about grammar or spelling, just get thoughts into words on a page.
  • Be honest and open with your thoughts as nobody is going to read your text
  • Do not pause to stare into space or to read what you've written. Write quickly but not in a hurry.
  • If you get off the topic or run out of ideas, keep writing anyway. If necessary,
    write nonsense or whatever comes into your head: anything to keep the words flowing.
  • If you feel bored or uncomfortable as you're writing, ask yourself what's bothering you and write about that.

Usually it is advised to write particularly on paper, since it affects the neural pathways differently,
but if it you feel better expressing yourself on your computer, you can use it as well.
